About Us

Who: We are two graduation ComputerScience majors! We LOVE raspberry pis and have been working with them for a good while now. This project was actually the brainchild of one of our favorite professors Gregory D Benson. We both love working with hardware and software so this project was right up our alley. Contact us if you have any questions; we're very down to earth people and want nothing more from this company then to make student and developer lives easier! Happy Clustering!

Why: We wanted an operating system that came with tools allowing for easy user implementation of hardware/software setups: Under the assumption that the user has little to no experience. Setting up a cluster may be trivial for some, but for others, it's a multi-day process of trying different setups and learning how things work. Our goal is to provide an environment where students and hobbyists can develop and learn any computer science concept with ease.

How: When you start up the cluster for the first time we wanted it to be as easy as possible to start developing, you actually don't need a monitor for this all you need is a wifi capable device (See set up video). From here you can connect and configure your cluster with the help of our built-in cluster tools. From here depending on what you want to learn/develop you can use some packages we have already installed for you: MPI, NFS, Docker, Fabric, ZeroRPC, Python, Java, C, PHP, Apache, SQL (More on request!)

Goals: Within a year we hope to have provided our first college with their rpiclusters and rpicluster specific cases! Rpiclusters are an amazing platform for students to develop on. Using our Wifi-Wifi network configuration students would be paying the same price as most textbooks to get a powerful development platform they can use throughout all 4 years of their Computer Science degree. This is game-changing, now all students are on the same platform and there is no worry about cross-platform conflicts between classes.

You're good to go !
